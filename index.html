Brain Computer Interfaces Are Here

In 1970, the National Science Foundation began research on implanting a computer chip directly into a human brain. This was followed by a contract from Defense Agency Research Projects Agency. After years of testing on animals, in the mid 1990s the first computer chips were implanted in humans. High-speed brain-computer interface is a computer chip implanted into the cerebral cortex allowing users to communicate with a computer wirelessly from their thoughts. Currently, the most common application of a BCI is the ability to control a computer cursor directly through thoughts. This functionality can be extrapolated into unimaginable applications. One of these applications gives paralized users the ability to control prosthetic limbs, and cure other ailments. “Neuralink Corporation is an American neurotechnology company founded by Elon Musk and others, developing implantable brain–machine interfaces (BMIs). The company was started in 2016 and was first publicly reported in March 2017” (Winkler). “Neuralink announced that it was working on a "sewing machine-like" device capable of implanting very thin threads into the brain, demonstrated a system that read information from a lab rat via 1500 electrodes (said to be 15 times more than the current maximum for systems used in human brains), and anticipated to start experiments with humans in 2020” (Markoff). This technology is already here, and will be improving at an exponential rate due to Artificial Intelligence.

Dangers of BCI

This technology has the potential for great good, and to cure once incurable problems. With that being said, the potential misuse of a poorly regulated high-speed brain-computer interface is unimaginable, and has far reaching consequences; these consequences far outweigh the possible derived advantages. If the technology can indeed read, and perhaps even write, neural data, then it would be able to upload and download ideas, theoretically. Some people speculate that “bad” thoughts could be deleted before the thinker was able to perceive them. Jack Ma, the founder of Alibaba, and Elon Musk participated in a debate in Shanghai. In 2019, during this debate, Jack Ma roughly said “we would teach machine all the bad things that bad guys want to do machine can learn quickly, and arrest all the bad guys immediately” (New China TV). The idea of “thought crimes” has an eerie dystopian theme. In addition to the reading and manipulation of thoughts, people speculate that once connected to the HSBC network users could be controlled as if they were puppets. Furthermore, with the emergence of this new technology what it means to be an individual will dramatically shift and change forever. The implications behind this have potentially dangerous consequences.

Solution

Brain Computer Interfaces are no longer a figment of the imagination. This technology is now a reality. A complete ban on this technology would not work. People that want this technology distributed to the world would work in silence and private; they will not listen to the law. Furthermore, this ban would be fighting a losing battle. The only foreseeable solution to all the foreseeable problems of a poorly implemented BCI would be to influence a cultural norm of data privacy. This technology has the potential for positive life changing value or being used to manipulate and suppress, and this is determined by who has the data. If we fail to create a culture of data privacy we will become salves to the masters who hold the data. With this data they will have controll over you. They will know you better than you know yourself. For these reasons, it is imperative and obvious that data privacy must be a top concern for every human.
